NHRA

Watch Live Youtube

NHRA is the highest tier of drag racing. It showcases the fastest-acceleration machines ever. The Top Class, “Top Fuel,” can accelerate from 0-530km/h in under 3.7 seconds across a 305 meter drag-strip! Events are held in an elimination style: 2 cars race per round, first to cross the line advances. It’s a sport of precision launches, engine brutality and massive flames.

      • Notable Manufacturers

      Chevrolet has dominated historically, winning the Manufacturers Cup many times (e.g. 27 times as of 2022)

      In 2024, Toyota won its first NHRA Manufacturers Cup, aided by strong performances across Top Fuel and Funny Car categories.

      Toyota’s presence is significant: it supports multiple teams (Kalitta Motorsports, Torrence Racing, AB Motorsports, SCAG, Ron Capps Motorsports, DC Motorsports) in NHRA’s top classes.

      However, Toyota plans to exit NHRA drag racing after the 2025 season.

      Dodge recently extended its partnership with Tony Stewart Racing (TSR) to field Dodge/SRT-backed dragsters and Funny Cars.

      In Pro Stock, over the decades many domestic brands have competed: Chevrolet, Ford, Chrysler, Pontiac, Oldsmobile, etc.

      Note: the Manufacturers Cup had traditionally excluded Top Fuel contributions, but in recent years its scope has expanded to include more classes.

      Notable Drivers

      Don Garlits (“Big Daddy”): a pioneer in drag racing engineering and one of the most influential figures. He helped popularize rear-engine Top Fuel dragsters to improve safety.

      Don Prudhomme (“The Snake”): legendary Funny Car driver; 4-time Funny Car champion (1975–1978).

      John Force: arguably the most famous NHRA Funny Car driver. He has amassed over 150 national event wins and multiple championships.

      Tony Schumacher: one of the greatest Top Fuel drivers. He’s an 8-time champion and has set many speed and elapsed time records.

      Antron Brown: a standout modern Top Fuel driver. He’s won multiple championships (2012, 2015, 2016, 2024) and is notable for being the first African American to win a Top Fuel championship.

      Ron Capps: top Funny Car driver, with multiple championships. He currently fields his own team (Ron Capps Motorsports).

      Cruz Pedregon: 2-time Funny Car champion, a long-standing name in the Funny Car ranks.

      Connie Kalitta: longtime drag racer (Top Fuel & Funny Car), and owner of Kalitta Motorsports. He was one of the first to break 200 mph in a National event.

      Bill Jenkins (“Grumpy” Jenkins): renowned engine-builder and car owner; influenced NHRA Pro Stock and engine development for decades.

      Roland Leong: owner and “tuner” known for his “Hawaiian” cars in Top Fuel and Funny Car classes.

      Steve Torrence — a dominant Top Fuel driver in recent years.

      Doug Kalitta — active in Top Fuel, with a long career and association with Kalitta Motorsports.

      Justin Ashley, Shawn Langdon, J.R. Todd — among the Toyota-supported drivers who have secured wins in recent seasons.

      Matt Hagan — a prominent Funny Car driver, often associated with Dodge / Mopar backing.

      Tony Stewart (in his NHRA role) — the former NASCAR champion has entered the NHRA drag racing scene, driving for the TSR / Dodge team in Top Fuel.

  • Funny Car Chandler Arizona 2018

    Funny Car of John Force blows up into a Fiery Flame

    During the Funny Car Nationals at Chandler Arizona, John Force showed great potential to reach the National Finals. Going into the Quarter Finals, his hopes came to a halt when his engine exploded into a ball of flame. This sudden force knocked John unconscious, sending him straight to the local hospital.
