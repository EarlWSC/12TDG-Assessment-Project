IMSA

Watch Live Youtube

IMSA is North America’s version of WEC, with cutting-edge technology, racing rivalry and multi-class drama on legendary circuits like Daytona, Sebring and Road Atlanta. Each being different lengths, they show drivers’ skill and perseverance. The difference between WEC and IMSA is the chassis; IMSA provides the chassis for teams racing in the GTP class.
There are 3 different classes that participate in the same race in IMSA:
GTP - Top Tier prototypes: chassis provided by manufacturers Dallara, Ligier Automotive, Multimatic and Oreca.
LMP2 - Spec-series prototypes; everyone has the same car with driver skill to account for winning.
GTD / Pro - Legal-sports cars with GT3 specs made for racing (There are 2 sub classes; GTD and GTD Pro. Pro are for the more experienced drivers who outpace the GTD drivers.)

      • Notable Drivers

      Hurley Haywood – 4× IMSA Champion; 5× Rolex 24 at Daytona winner; 3× Le Mans winner; 2× Sebring winner; endurance racing icon.

      Al Holbert – 7× IMSA Champion; record 49 IMSA race wins; victories at Daytona, Sebring, and Le Mans.

      Scott Pruett – 9× IMSA Champion; 5× overall Rolex 24 winner; 10× class wins at Daytona; 3× Sebring winner; 60 career IMSA victories.

      Nick Tandy – Porsche factory driver; first to win the “Big Six” endurance races (Daytona, Le Mans, Sebring, Spa, Nürburgring, Petit Le Mans); historic Grand Slam in 2025.

      Ben Keating – LMP2 standout; multiple Rolex 24, Sebring, Watkins Glen, and Road America wins; Michelin Endurance Cup and IMSA LMP2 champion.

      John Farano – 2022 IMSA LMP2 Drivers’ Champion; 5 wins and 14 podiums since 2020.


      Notable Manufacturers


      Porsche – Consistent winner in GT and prototypes; factory effort through Porsche Penske Motorsport.

      Acura – Major GTP contender with top drivers (Alex Palou, Scott Dixon, Felix Rosenqvist).

      Cadillac – Longstanding IMSA competitor, including entries from Chip Ganassi Racing.

      Dallara, ORECA, Riley, Multimatic – Key chassis suppliers supporting IMSA prototypes and GT entries.
