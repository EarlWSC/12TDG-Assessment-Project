WRC
World Rally Championship

Watch Live Youtube

The World Rally Championship (WRC) is the top level of International Rally Racing. It features drivers racing each other against the clock on tricky environments like gravel, snow, tarmac and dirt roads across remote regions of the world.
WRC uses Rally1 Hybrid specifications. It combines a 1.6L Turbocharged Engine with a Hybrid-electric system. They race against the clock in special stages – closed public roads where the driver and co-driver must aim for the lowest time from Start to Finish.
If you’ve come looking for precision, adaptability and split-second teamwork decisions, then you have come to watch the right thing.

      Sébastien Loeb - 9x World Rally Champion for Citroën (2004–2012)

      Sébastien Ogier - 8x World Rally Champion for Volkswagen, M-Sport Ford, and Toyota (2013–2021)

      Kalle Rovanperä - 1x World Rally Champion for Toyota (2022), youngest ever WRC champion

      Tommi Mäkinen - 4x World Rally Champion for Mitsubishi (1996–1999)

      Colin McRae - 1995 World Rally Champion for Subaru, iconic figure in rallying


      Manufacturers


      Lancia - 10x World Manufacturers' Champions, most successful WRC manufacturer (1983–1992)

      Citroën - 8x World Manufacturers' Champions, dominant in the Loeb era (2003–2012)

      Toyota - 5x World Manufacturers' Champions, current powerhouse in WRC (1993, 1994, 1999, 2018, 2021)

      Ford - 3x World Manufacturers' Champions, long-standing WRC participant (1979, 2006, 2007)

      Peugeot - 3x World Manufacturers' Champions, Group B legend (1985, 1986, 2000)

      Subaru - 3x World Manufacturers' Champions, known for the Impreza era (1995–1997)

  • Historical Photo of Group B

    When there’s no rules, that applies to the crowd also.

    During the 80s in WRC, a Rally stage called Group B was created into fruition with no rules, other than to be the quickest rally car. The rules also applied to the audience also, who had the balls of steel and guts of iron to spectate a car hurling towards them at +150km/h. Only a few meters away from the side and one mistake from a catastrophic crash, it was inevitable for Group B to come to a close to improve safety.

  • Italian GP 2021

    Max ontop of Lewis, both out of the Race!

    As Lewis Hamilton was leaving the pits, he was caught by the charging redbull of Max Verstappen, in which they were side by side into the first Chicane in Monza 2021. As they took the corner, both didn’t want to give up position. So eager to keep the position, Max Verstappen took a sausage kerb that caused the car to erratically jump into the air and, being so close to Lewis, landed on top of his Mercedes. They both didn’t finish the race, with previous Mclaren driver Ricciardo taking the win.

  • Bahrain GP 2018

    3 cars on the same corner! Who comes out on top?

    As the Racing Point of Ocon was attacking Renault Driver Hulkenberg, unbeknownst to them, a charging Lewis Hamilton was fast approaching. Needing to make a decision, he took the deep inside line and joined the braking point of Turn 1 with 2 other cars side by side. In the end, Lewis Hamilton came out on top, with Ocon following close behind and Hulkenberg distancing away.
