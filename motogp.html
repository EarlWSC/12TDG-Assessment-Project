MotoGP

Watch Live Youtube

MotoGP is the premier class of motorcycle road racing, sanctioned by the FIM. It features the top of the class, fastest and most technologically advanced motorcycles that have been created. These motorcycles, though small and timid, are capable of incredible speeds of 360km/h. MotoGP bikes are prototypes and are not bound from any production based bikes. With a 1,000cc four-stroke engine producing 250 horsepower, a shocking dry weight of under 160kg, these motorcycles can show an incredible side of dangerous and intense racing. Elbows, knees and shoulders scraping the ground below through winding corners and fast straights. It requires the most bravery, balance and precision to withstand this sport.

Qatar Grand Prix 2015

  • A Burning rivalry continues to grill at Qatar between Marquez and Rossi

    In a race where the championship rivalry was burning to a critical point, any position higher than your rival would mean less weight in the next race. This race proved great resilience and courage from Defending Champion Valentino Rossi and Runner-Up Marc Marquez. Battling corner to corner and switching places left and right, Valentino Rossi showed his defensive masterclass and proved why he was a previous multi-championship winner.

    • Catalyuna Grand Prix 2009

  • Rossi vs Lorenzo's Epic Penultimate & Final Lap Battle

    One of the more Historic races in MotoGP history, the 2009 Catalyuna Grand Prix had one of the most intense final laps in MotoGP history. With 2 laps to go, Jorge Lorenzo was leading the race with Valentino Rossi in close behind in 2nd. Rossi was determined to take the lead and committed a daring overtake into turn 1 which gave him the lead for now. Coming into the final lap, Lorenzo was determined to take back the lead and did so with a daring move into turn 1. The two riders swapped positions multiple times throughout the lap, with Rossi making a daring move into turn 10 to take the lead once again. However, Lorenzo was not done yet and made a final attempt to take back the lead into turn 12. In a thrilling finish, Rossi held back Lorenzo's intimidating overtake, making it to the finish line first and winning the race by a mere 0.142 seconds.

      • Notable Riders

      Giacomo Agostini – 8-time premier-class World Champion (1966–1972, 1975); most titles in MotoGP history.

      Valentino Rossi – 7-time premier-class World Champion (2001–2005, 2008–2009); one of the most iconic figures in the sport.

      Marc Márquez – 6-time premier-class World Champion (2013–2014, 2016–2019); known for dominating Honda era and remarkable comebacks.

      Joan Mir – MotoGP World Champion in 2020, riding for Suzuki; the first Suzuki rider to win since 2000.

      Fabio Quartararo – MotoGP World Champion in 2021 for Yamaha; one of the new generation of title winners.

      Francesco Bagnaia – MotoGP World Champion in 2022 and 2023 for Ducati; representing the current era of dominance.

      Jorge Martín – MotoGP World Champion in 2024 with Pramac Racing; adds to the depth of champions now active on the grid.


      Notable Manufacturers


      Honda – Most successful MotoGP constructor with 25 premier-class titles and over 300 premier-class race wins.

      MV Agusta – Historic powerhouse with 16 constructors’ titles, dominant in the 1960s and early 1970s.

      Yamaha – Major force with 14 constructors’ titles and second-highest total wins after Honda.

      Ducati – Modern-day giant, with six constructors’ titles; recent dominance with five consecutive rider titles (2020–2024) and near-total race control in 2024.

      Suzuki – Notable accomplishments including rider and constructor titles, with champions like Joan Mir.

Dreher had nowhere to go after Morrison went down as Treston was unfortunately parked and crashed in the middle of a blind downward corner.
